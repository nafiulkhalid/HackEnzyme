Gene Therapy

Our Enzyme Sequence Predictor uses machine learning to predict enzyme sequences based on structural inputs, with wide-ranging impacts:

  1. Drug Discovery: Treatment Selection CYP2D6 Many medications, including opioids and antidepressants, are metabolized by CYP2D6. Drug selection and dosage may be impacted by patients with specific CYP2D6 genetic variations who metabolize medications either too slowly (poor metabolizers) or too quickly (ultra-rapid metabolizers). Our model could treat this by providing customized enzyme adjustments or alternate treatment options, maximizing medication efficacy and reducing side effects.
  2. Gene Therapy: Immunogenicity Asparaginase is an enzyme used to treat lymphoblastic leukemia. It is most commonly used for its ability to break down asparagine, an important amino acid chain for the cancer cell. Although the enzyme is potent, its immunogenic properties makes it unsuitable for longer-term treatment. This is because the enzyme flags patients’ immune systems, making it think that the enzyme is a threat, leading to further implications. Our model is able to create enzymes similar in attributes to Asparaginase, while leaving out keywords that would make it immunogenic. Citation: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10213690/
  3. Biotechnology: In biofuel production, food processing, and more, predicting enzyme variants helps industries optimize processes, enhancing efficiency under specific conditions.
  4. Personalized Medicine: Prolyl Endopeptidases Gluten’s resistance to digestion may cause complications; projects have proven enzyme solutions are able to improve both digestion and immune responses. Our model could develop similar enzyme solutions, predicted from the smiles chemical compound string, to aid in food allergy. Citations: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7996948/

This tool accelerates innovation in medicine, biotech, and industry, driving real-world advancements by connecting enzyme research to practical applications.

What We Do

At Enzyme Sequence Predictor, we use advanced algorithms and machine learning models to predict the sequence of amino acids in enzymes based on various inputs such as enzyme structures, keywords, subtraits, and more. Our tool is aimed at helping researchers and students better understand the structures of enzymes and their functions.

How To Use It

Using our tool is simple:

  1. Upload Enzyme Structure: Upload a PDB format file containing the structure of the enzyme.
  2. Add Keywords: Provide keywords that describe the function of the enzyme, such as 'catalysis', 'substrate', or 'cofactor'.
  3. Enter Subtraits: Specify any subtraits or additional information about the enzyme's behavior, such as temperature sensitivity, binding affinities, etc.
  4. Product Details: Optionally, add information about the product or outcome of the enzyme’s function.
  5. Get Predicted Sequence: After hitting the "Predict Sequence" button, you will receive the predicted sequence of amino acids for the enzyme. The output will also visualize the enzyme structure.
